"The New Federalist Party does not seek to reinvent America, but to remember it — the founding bargain, the covenant between the governed and those who govern, the premise that public service is a form of sacrifice, not extraction."

I. Preamble — The Call to Service

We reject the politics of the loudest voice in favor of the politics of the best idea. We reject grievance as platform, nostalgia as policy, and spectacle as governance. Our mission: to ensure that the federal government remains what Lincoln called "a last best hope" — and what Lincoln's America was never quite able to fully become.

The New Federalist Party believes in competence, in the patient work of institutions, in the radical notion that the people who administer the republic should be, above all else, good at it.

II. Domestic Policy — Architecture of Opportunity

  • Education Tuition-free public university for students who maintain a service-grade GPA. Knowledge is infrastructure.
  • Civil Covenant Judicial appointments drawn exclusively from a pool of candidates with demonstrated record of impartiality. The bench is not a reward for loyalty.
  • Healthcare Architecture A public option administered with the rigor of a flight safety board. No one should go bankrupt for staying alive.
  • Infrastructure The nation's physical plant — roads, bridges, water systems, power grids — treated as a long-term capital investment, not a campaign prop. Boring, important, done.
  • Housing Federal land-use reform to break the zoning cartels that have made American cities unaffordable. The dream of owning a modest home should not require generational wealth.

III. Foreign Policy — Responsible Sovereignty

  • Alliances Recommitment to treaty obligations as binding law, not conditional favor. Allies are not leverage; they are architecture.
  • Intelligence Separation of the intelligence community from political directorate. The CRM114 discriminator passes only verified transmission — so should our foreign policy apparatus.
  • Aid and Engagement Foreign assistance tied explicitly to democratic governance benchmarks. We do not fund autocracy in the name of stability.
  • Military An armed force sized for actual defense — not projection of dominance, not theater, not the enrichment of contractors. Lethal when required. Restrained as a first principle.

IV. Electoral Integrity — The Transmission

The signal is only as good as the channel. The New Federalist Party supports:

  • Independent Redistricting All congressional maps drawn by nonpartisan commissions with published methodology. The map is not the territory; it should not be weaponized as one.
  • Ranked-Choice Voting Federal elections conducted by ranked-choice method to eliminate the spoiler effect and the lesser-evil calculus that has demoralized the electorate for forty years.
  • Paper Ballots Every vote cast on paper. Every paper retained. The simplest systems are the hardest to corrupt.
  • Campaign Finance Strict aggregate limits on political donations with public matching for small-dollar contributions. Democracy should not be a subscription service.

V. The Record

The New Federalist Party has not yet won an election. It has, however, been right about most things, which counts for something in the long run and nothing in the short.
